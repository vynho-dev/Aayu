## 1. Product Vision

**Aayu** (आयु — "lifespan, vitality") is an operating system for the patient's side of healthcare in India.

Every other party in Indian healthcare has software built for them — hospitals have EMRs, insurers have claims engines, the government has scheme portals. The patient, whose body and money are actually on the line, has a plastic folder of paper. Aayu turns that folder into a living asset that fights for the family's money, follows them to every doctor, claims what they're owed, and guards their health for life.

**Vision statement:** *Your family's entire health history, working for you — for your money, your care, and your peace of mind.*

The long-term product is a comprehensive personal healthcare assistant. The MVP is the wedge that earns trust and captures the data: recovering money on wrongly rejected insurance claims.

---

## 2. Problem Statement

Indian families face three compounding, connected problems:

1. **Insurance claims are wrongly denied at scale.** Insurers repudiated roughly ₹26,000 crore of health claims in FY24. Independent surveys indicate only about a quarter of policyholders get a claim fully approved, while roughly a third are rejected outright — often on invalid or contestable grounds. Patients rarely appeal because policies are dense legalese and the grievance process is opaque. This hits hardest during a hospitalization — the worst week of a family's life.

2. **Medical history is fragmented and lost.** When patients change hospitals or doctors, their history doesn't travel with them. Records live in scattered paper files, WhatsApp photos, and siloed hospital systems. Patients repeat expensive tests and re-explain their history from scratch, every time.

3. **Government health entitlements go unclaimed.** Citizens are eligible for schemes and benefits they never discover, because eligibility is buried in bureaucracy and nobody maps a person's actual medical/financial profile to what they're owed.

These are not three markets. They are three symptoms of one gap: **no system is built for the patient's own side.**

---

## 3. Mission & Goals

**Mission:** Put every Indian family in control of their health data, their health money, and their health decisions.

**Strategic goals (12–18 months):**

- **G1 — Trust wedge:** Become the default way Indians fight a wrongly rejected health-insurance claim.
- **G2 — Data capture:** Convert every claim interaction into a structured, longitudinal health record (the Vault), captured as a byproduct of the wedge — never via cold data-entry.
- **G3 — Expansion:** Use the same data + AI engine to surface unclaimed government entitlements.
- **G4 — Retention:** Turn one-time crisis users into recurring users via continuity-of-care value.

**MVP goals (first release, 4-day build):**

- **M1:** A family member can upload a rejected claim and receive a real, clause-cited appeal letter generated by AI.
- **M2:** Documents uploaded for the claim automatically populate a real Vault for the patient.
- **M3:** The platform visibly surfaces one relevant government scheme and a plain-language policy explainer, proving the "one document, three jobs" thesis.

## 4. Target Users & Personas

The MVP is built for **one primary persona**. Others are documented for context but are **not** targeted in the MVP.

### 4.1 Primary persona (MVP) — "The Caregiving Adult Child"

- **Name:** Priya, 34, works in a Tier-1 city; her parents live in a Tier-2 town.
- **Context:** Her father (62) was recently hospitalized. The cashless claim was partially denied at discharge. She is the one who collects documents, calls the insurer, and pays the shortfall.
- **Goals:** Recover the denied amount; stop losing her father's medical history; not feel helpless from another city.
- **Pain:** Doesn't understand the policy; doesn't know appeals are possible; juggling this remotely.
- **Why she matters:** She is motivated, digitally capable, and financially responsible for the outcome — the ideal wedge user. **The account holder is the caregiver; the patient is the parent.**

### 4.2 Secondary personas (context only — NOT MVP targets)

- **The Patient (parent)** — the subject of the record; may be low-digital-literacy.
- **Government agencies** — scheme uptake is their KPI; a future funding/partner channel.
- **Insurers / Employers / Hospitals / Labs** — future B2B2C channels (Phase 3+).

## 5. Core Value Proposition

**For** families managing a parent's healthcare **who** face a wrongly rejected insurance claim, **Aayu** is a patient-side health platform **that** uses AI to fight the claim, recover the money, and quietly build a lifelong health record — **unlike** insurers, aggregators, or hospitals, which are structurally on the other side of the table.

**Three one-line value props by module:**

- **Insurance Advisor:** "Your claim was rejected. We fight it — and you pay only if we win."
- **Vault:** "Your parent's entire medical history, in one place, that follows them to every doctor."
- **Scheme Advisor:** "You're owed benefits you never knew existed. We find them."

**The defensible core:** One document, ingested once, does three jobs — claim evidence, vault record, and eligibility signal. Competitors building any single module cannot match this on cost or coherence.

## 6. User Journeys

### 6.1 MVP hero journey (must work end-to-end)

1. Priya signs up and creates a profile for her father (the patient).
2. She is prompted to upload the claim rejection letter, the policy document, and hospital bills/discharge summary.
3. Aayu's AI reads the documents (including scanned photos), extracts a structured Health Graph, and identifies why the claim was likely wrongly denied.
4. Aayu generates a clause-cited appeal letter she can download and send.
5. The extracted data auto-populates her father's Vault (conditions, medications, timeline).
6. Aayu surfaces one relevant government scheme and offers a plain-language "ask anything about this policy" chat.

### 6.2 Supporting flows (MVP, lighter)

- **Explain my policy:** Priya asks free-text questions about the uploaded policy; AI answers in plain language.
- **View Vault:** Priya browses the auto-generated record for her father.

### 6.3 Future journeys (NOT in MVP)

- Pre-claim check before filing; proactive health alerts; medication management; multi-family-member management; second-opinion prep; provider-facing record sharing.

## 7. Functional Requirements

IDs are stable references for agents. **[MVP]** = in first release. **[FUTURE]** = deferred.

**Authentication & accounts**
- FR-1 **[MVP]** User can sign up / log in with email + password (via managed auth).
- FR-2 **[MVP]** User (caregiver) can create and select a Patient profile they manage.
- FR-3 **[MVP]** All data (documents, claims, records) is scoped to a Patient owned by the User.
- FR-4 **[FUTURE]** Multiple caregivers per patient; patient self-access; OTP/mobile login.

**Document ingestion**
- FR-5 **[MVP]** User can upload documents (PDF and image formats) for a selected patient.
- FR-6 **[MVP]** System accepts scanned photos and multi-page PDFs; stores files encrypted.
- FR-7 **[MVP]** AI extracts structured data from documents into a Health Graph object.
- FR-8 **[FUTURE]** Direct fetch from ABDM/ABHA national health rails; email-forwarding ingestion.

**Insurance Claim Advisor (hero)**
- FR-9 **[MVP]** User uploads rejection letter + policy + bills; AI analyzes the denial.
- FR-10 **[MVP]** AI produces an assessment: likely reason for denial + whether it's contestable.
- FR-11 **[MVP]** AI generates a clause-cited appeal letter, downloadable as PDF.
- FR-12 **[MVP]** "Explain my policy" — free-text Q&A against the uploaded policy.
- FR-13 **[FUTURE]** Pre-claim check (avoid rejection before filing).
- FR-14 **[FUTURE]** Claim status tracking; IRDAI Ombudsman escalation workflow; success-fee billing.

**Medical Records Vault**
- FR-15 **[MVP]** Vault auto-populates from documents uploaded during the claim flow.
- FR-16 **[MVP]** Vault displays conditions, medications, allergies, and a chronological timeline.
- FR-17 **[FUTURE]** AI trend detection; drug-interaction flags; manual record entry; sharing to providers.

**Government Scheme Advisor**
- FR-18 **[MVP]** System surfaces at least one relevant scheme based on extracted profile data (rule-based match against a small curated scheme list).
- FR-19 **[FUTURE]** Full eligibility engine; document assistance; application submission; agency integration.

**Cross-cutting**
- FR-20 **[MVP]** Consent screen shown before first document upload.
- FR-21 **[MVP]** Graceful handling when AI cannot read a document or returns low confidence.

## 8. Non-Functional Requirements

- NFR-1 **Security:** All documents encrypted at rest (S3 SSE) and in transit (TLS). No raw medical data written to application logs.
- NFR-2 **Privacy (DPDP basics):** Explicit consent captured before processing; data scoped per patient; deletion path documented (even if manual for MVP).
- NFR-3 **Performance:** Document analysis returns within ~60 seconds for a typical claim set; UI shows progress state during processing.
- NFR-4 **Reliability:** AI failure never crashes the flow; user sees a clear fallback and can retry.
- NFR-5 **Usability:** Primary flow completable by a non-technical caregiver on mobile web; minimal typing.
- NFR-6 **Scalability (design intent):** Serverless/managed services so the MVP can scale without re-architecture; not load-tested in MVP.
- NFR-7 **Maintainability:** AI prompts and the scheme list are stored as editable config, not hardcoded in business logic.
- NFR-8 **Accessibility:** Legible font sizes, high contrast, works on low-end Android browsers.

## 9. System Overview

**Architecture (managed/serverless-first):**

- **Frontend:** React (mobile-web-first, responsive).
- **Backend:** Python service exposing REST endpoints.
- **Auth:** Managed identity provider (e.g., AWS Cognito) — do not hand-roll auth.
- **Storage:** Object storage for documents (S3, encrypted); relational DB (Postgres/RDS) for structured data.
- **AI layer:** Cloud LLM API (Claude/OpenAI) with multimodal/vision for scanned documents.
- **Hosting:** Managed hosting (S3/CloudFront + ECS Fargate + Lambda). No Kubernetes or bespoke network topology in MVP; use only the minimal private networking required by managed RDS/Redis.

**Core data model (authoritative for MVP):**

```
User (account holder = caregiver)
  └── manages → Patient (e.g., parent)
                  ├── Document (uploaded files, in S3)
                  ├── Claim (rejection analysis + generated appeal)
                  ├── HealthGraph (extracted structured data)
                  └── SchemeMatch (surfaced entitlement)
```

**Key principle:** Every Document/Claim/HealthGraph/SchemeMatch belongs to a Patient, and every Patient belongs to a User. This relationship is fixed on Day 1 and must not be refactored later.

## 10. AI Capabilities

Aayu uses **one AI engine with three "heads"** — not three separate AI products. The engine converts messy, multilingual, potentially handwritten documents into a structured **Health Graph**, which then serves all modules.

- **Extraction head [MVP]:** Document → structured Health Graph (conditions, meds, allergies, procedures, dates, amounts, policy terms).
- **Claims head [MVP]:** Health Graph + policy + rejection → denial assessment + clause-cited appeal letter.
- **Policy Q&A head [MVP]:** Free-text questions answered against the uploaded policy.
- **Entitlements head [MVP-lite]:** Rule-based match of profile against a curated scheme list.
- **Care head [FUTURE]:** Trend detection, drug-interaction flags, proactive alerts.

**AI requirements:**
- Must handle image inputs (scanned/photographed documents) natively.
- Must return structured JSON for programmatic use (strict output format).
- Must express confidence; low-confidence extractions trigger the fallback path (FR-21).
- Prompts are versioned config, editable without code changes.

## 11. 🔒 LOCKED MVP SPECIFICATION (CONTRACT)

> **This section is binding. AI agents and engineers must not exceed it. Anything not listed here is out of scope for the first release. When in doubt, build less.**

### 11.1 MVP Screens/Pages (exhaustive — no others)

1. **Landing page** — what Aayu does, single primary CTA (Get Started).
2. **Auth screen** — sign up / log in (email + password, managed provider).
3. **Patient selector / create-patient** — caregiver picks or creates the parent's profile.
4. **Consent screen** — one-time consent before first upload.
5. **Upload screen** — upload rejection letter, policy, bills for the selected patient; progress state.
6. **Claim result screen** — denial assessment + generated appeal letter + download button.
7. **Policy Q&A** — chat box to ask questions about the uploaded policy (can be a panel on the claim result screen).
8. **Vault screen** — auto-populated record: conditions, medications, allergies, timeline.
9. **Scheme match** — one surfaced scheme with a short explanation (can be a card on the Vault or dashboard).

*Total: a maximum of 9 distinct views. A simple dashboard/home may tie them together.*

### 11.2 Features included (exhaustive)

- Email/password auth (FR-1).
- Create/select one patient per action (FR-2, FR-3).
- One-time consent (FR-20).
- Upload PDF + image documents, encrypted storage (FR-5, FR-6).
- AI extraction to Health Graph (FR-7).
- AI denial assessment + clause-cited appeal letter, downloadable as PDF (FR-9, FR-10, FR-11).
- Plain-language policy Q&A (FR-12).
- Auto-populated Vault view (FR-15, FR-16).
- One rule-based scheme match (FR-18).
- AI-failure fallback (FR-21).

### 11.3 MVP User Flows

- **Flow A (hero):** Sign up → create patient → consent → upload claim docs → AI analysis → view assessment + download appeal → (data seeds Vault + scheme).
- **Flow B:** From claim result, ask policy questions in plain language.
- **Flow C:** Open Vault → view auto-generated record and timeline for the patient.

### 11.4 Acceptance Criteria

- **AC-1:** A new user can sign up, create a patient, and reach the upload screen without help.
- **AC-2:** Uploading a rejection letter + policy + bills produces a denial assessment naming a plausible reason.
- **AC-3:** The system generates a downloadable appeal letter that references at least one specific policy clause and the patient's details.
- **AC-4:** Documents uploaded in the claim flow appear as structured entries in the Vault without re-entry.
- **AC-5:** At least one scheme is shown when the profile matches curated criteria; otherwise a clear "no match yet" state.
- **AC-6:** Policy Q&A returns a relevant plain-language answer grounded in the uploaded policy.
- **AC-7:** If AI cannot read a document, the user sees a clear message and can retry — the app does not crash.
- **AC-8:** All documents are stored encrypted; no raw medical text appears in logs.
- **AC-9:** The full hero flow works on mobile web.

### 11.5 Edge Cases (must be handled in MVP)

- Blurry / low-quality photo of a document → low-confidence path + retry prompt.
- Document in a regional language → attempt extraction; if unsupported, clear message.
- Missing document (e.g., no policy uploaded) → proceed with what's available, flag what's missing.
- Non-medical / wrong file uploaded → detect and ask user to re-upload.
- AI/API timeout or error → fallback message, no crash, retry option.
- Multi-page PDF and multi-file upload → all processed.
- No scheme match → explicit empty state, not a blank screen.

### 11.6 Technical Boundaries

- Stack fixed: **React + Python + AWS managed services + cloud LLM API.**
- Auth via managed provider only; no custom auth.
- Serverless/managed hosting only; **no Kubernetes or self-managed data services.** A minimal managed VPC for RDS and optional Redis is allowed.
- EKS is a post-MVP deployment option only when multiple independently scaled workloads or specialized scheduling justify it.
- Scheme matching is **rule-based against a small curated list** — not a full eligibility engine.
- Success-fee billing/payments are **not** built in MVP.
- No native mobile app — mobile web only.

### 11.7 🚫 Out of Scope / NOT Building (authoritative)

The following are explicitly **excluded** from the MVP. Do not build, stub only if listed as visible:

- ❌ Payments, billing, or success-fee collection.
- ❌ Pre-claim check ("avoid rejection before filing").
- ❌ Full government eligibility engine or application submission.
- ❌ ABDM/ABHA integration or any external health-record fetch.
- ❌ Claim status tracking / Ombudsman escalation workflow.
- ❌ AI trend detection, drug-interaction flags, proactive health alerts.
- ❌ Medication management / reminders.
- ❌ Multiple caregivers per patient; patient self-login; role management.
- ❌ Provider/insurer/employer-facing views or B2B2C features.
- ❌ Native iOS/Android apps.
- ❌ Manual record entry UI (Vault is auto-populated only in MVP).
- ❌ Notifications (email/SMS/push).
- ❌ Analytics dashboards beyond basic success metrics instrumentation.
- ❌ Multi-language UI (content can be English-only in MVP; document *content* may be multilingual).

### 11.8 Future Enhancements (deferred, not MVP)

Pre-claim check; full eligibility + application assistance; ABDM integration; success-fee payments; trend/interaction AI; medication management; proactive Guardian alerts; multi-caregiver; provider sharing; native apps; insurer/employer B2B2C.

## 12. Success Metrics

**MVP (validation) metrics:**
- Activation: % of signups that complete the hero flow (target ≥ 40%).
- Core value: % of claims that produce a downloadable appeal (target ≥ 90% of valid uploads).
- Quality: % of appeals users rate as "usable" (thumbs up).
- Data capture: avg. structured records auto-added to Vault per user.
- Retention signal: % of users who return to view the Vault within 7 days.

**North-star (long-term):** Total money recovered for families (₹). Secondary: number of lifelong health records under management.

## 13. Assumptions

- Users will upload real medical/insurance documents if the payoff (recovered money) is clear.
- Cloud LLM APIs can extract usable structure from scanned Indian medical/insurance documents.
- A crisis-first wedge captures data that cold-start record apps cannot.
- ABDM/ABHA rails will exist for a future Vault upgrade (verify before Phase 1).
- Caregivers, not patients, are the willing early adopters and payers.

## 14. Constraints

- **Time:** 4-day build for the first release.
- **Team:** Small; AI coding agent does most implementation, human reviews.
- **Regulatory:** Must position as claim *assistance*, not legal practice or insurance advice; DPDP basics required from Day 1.
- **Budget:** Cloud API + AWS managed services; keep infra minimal.

## 15. Risks

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Messy/handwritten docs break extraction | High | Multimodal vision model; confidence + retry fallback (FR-21) |
| Scope creep kills the 4-day timeline | High | Section 11 as binding contract; strict "not building" list |
| AWS setup consumes build time | Med | Managed/serverless only; swap any piece that fights >1 hr |
| DPDP/health-data sensitivity | High | Encryption, consent, no raw-data logging from Day 1 |
| AI generates wrong legal/clause claims | High | Ground answers in uploaded docs; label as assistance, not advice; human review before sending |
| Trust positioning compromised (seen as insurer's tool) | High (long-term) | Always patient-side; never build denial tooling |

## 18. Glossary

- **Aayu:** The product; patient-side healthcare OS.
- **Wedge:** The first, high-pain entry feature (Insurance Claim Advisor) that captures data and trust.
- **Vault:** The patient's auto-populated lifelong medical record.
- **Health Graph:** The structured data object (conditions, meds, allergies, procedures, dates) extracted from documents.
- **Caregiver / Account holder:** The User — typically an adult child managing a parent's care.
- **Patient:** The subject of the record — typically the parent.
- **Scheme:** A government health entitlement/benefit.
- **ABDM / ABHA:** India's national digital health mission / health ID rails.
- **DPDP:** India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act.
- **Head (AI):** One task-specific function of the shared AI engine (extraction, claims, policy Q&A, entitlements).
