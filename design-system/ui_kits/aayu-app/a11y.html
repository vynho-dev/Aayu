Accessibility · WCAG 2.1 Level AA
Accessibility audit
Aayu serves caregivers of all ages on low-end phones, often in bright sun or low light. Accessibility isn't a checkbox here — it's core to the "warmth + evidence" promise. This audits the design system and reference screens against WCAG 2.1 AA.
01 · Summary

Where the system stands

The design system's tokens and components were built to meet AA from the start. Colour, focus, motion, and semantics were verified against the 30 applicable Level A/AA success criteria. Items marked "manual" need a real device + screen-reader pass once the app is coded — the design system can't fully guarantee them.

03 · Findings & fixes

Issues caught and resolved

Raised during the audit and already addressed in tokens/components.

Focus was invisible on teal surfaces

A single teal focus ring disappeared on the teal-tinted success cards and the primary button.

→ Fixed: focus ring is a 2px ring plus a 2px white offset (--focus-ring + outline-offset), visible on both light and teal backgrounds.

Count-up animation ignored reduced-motion

The ₹-amount count-up on the claim result animated regardless of the user's OS setting.

→ Fixed: ClaimResultScreen checks prefers-reduced-motion and snaps straight to the final value; the global token disables non-essential transitions too.

Status colour carried meaning alone

Early status chips distinguished "attention" vs "success" by fill colour only.

→ Fixed: every StatusChip pairs a Lucide icon + text with its tone; colour is reinforcement, never the sole signal (1.4.1).

Processing step wasn't announced

Screen-reader users got no feedback while "Reading the policy… / Finding the clause…" cycled.

→ Fixed: the processing view is role="status" aria-live="polite"; the spinner is aria-hidden so only the step text is read.

Icon-only back buttons had no accessible name

The arrow-left buttons in Vault and Policy Q&A were unlabelled.

→ Fixed: icon-only controls carry an aria-label ("Go back", "Close"); decorative glyphs use aria-hidden.

05 · Assistive tech & motion

Behaviour built in

  • Keyboard: everything actionable is a native control — full tab traversal, Enter/Space activation, no div click handlers.
  • Focus ring: a single shared token; never removed, only styled. Visible on light and teal.
  • Screen reader: semantic landmarks, one h1 per screen, labelled forms, live region for processing.
  • Reduced motion: prefers-reduced-motion disables the count-up and non-essential transitions globally.
  • Target size: 44px minimum, enforced by button/list-row/tab-bar component heights.
  • Zoom: layout reflows to 200% without horizontal scroll; no maximum-scale lock.

Manual verification once coded

Four items can't be fully guaranteed at design-system stage — verify on real hardware during the build.

Screen readerFull pass with TalkBack (Android) + VoiceOver (iOS): reading order, that the appeal-letter editor and bottom sheet are announced, and that the live region isn't over-chatty.
